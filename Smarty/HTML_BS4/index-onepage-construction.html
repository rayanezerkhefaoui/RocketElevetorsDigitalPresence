Tallest building in Queens closes in on completion

The tallest building in Queens, NYC, at 68 stories and 778 ft, Skyline Tower is essentially complete, with New York YIMBY describing finishing touches going onto it. Located at 23-15 44th Drive in Long Island City, the tower was designed by Hill West Architects and developed by United Construction & Development Group, FSA Capital and Risland US Holdings LLC.

PLANS PROGRESS FOR MIXED-USE TOWER IN CHICAGO’S WEST LOOP

Plans for a 27-story mixed-use building at 160 North Elizabeth Street in Chicago's West Loop area are progressing, Chicago YIMBY reported on January 11. Developed by Moceri + Roszak and designed by Thomas Roszak Architecture, the plan calls for a 293-ft-tall tower with 383 residential units, 9,000 ft2 of ground-level commercial space, and parking slots for 150 bicycles and 117 vehicles.

CIBES LIFT GROUP EXPANDS SWEDISH FOOTPRINT WITH ACQUISITION

Cibes Lift Group, a Swedish manufacturer specializing in low-speed lifts, expanded its domestic footprint with the acquisition of Upplands Hiss AB, Cibes announced on January 12. Based in Uppsala in central Sweden, Upplands Hiss has approximately 10 employees who service roughly 300 lifts.

ZHA WINS DESIGN COMPETITION FOR SHENZHEN TOWER PROJECT

Zaha Hadid Architects (ZHA) has won a competition to design Tower C, a stunning twin tower building at Shenzhen Bay Super Headquarters in China, designboom reported on January 11. Tower C, which will integrate clusters of corporate headquarters serving Guangdong, Hong Kong and Macau, will accommodate 300,000 employees each day and include venues for conferences, exhibitions and other cultural programming.

AEG ACQUIRES TWO NEW JERSEY-BASED ELEVATOR COMPANIES

American Elevator Group (AEG) has completed the acquisitions of Standard Elevator Corp., based in Clifton, New Jersey, and Dura-Lift Elevator, based in Sayreville, New Jersey, the company announced on January 11. Lift Business Advisors facilitated the transactions on behalf of Standard and Dura-Lift. Founded in 1979, Standard Elevator is an independent provider of elevator maintenance, modernization and installation in New Jersey and the NYC metropolitan area.

DEVELOPER PLANS FIRST SKYSCRAPER IN MÉRIDA, MEXICO

A developer is planning to build The Sky, the first skyscraper in Mérida, Mexico, the Council on Tall Buildings and Urban Habitat reported on January 7. The mixed-use tower is to stand 160 m and provide 35,000 m2 for offices and clinics, 300 m2 for commercial space and 2,500 m2 for restaurants. The proposal envisions 39 or 40 stories, a rooftop terrace and restaurant, meeting space, a wellness center and six levels of parking.
